About Us

AFLAMEii was formed by individuals who have been fighting and recovering from lyme disease, mold toxicity/biotoxin illness/CIRS, and/or environmental intolerance/multiple chemical sensitivity, along with medical doctors and researchers who are experts in these illnesses.

The founders believe that nobody facing these conditions should be denied diagnosis or treatment due to their personal financial situation.


Mission Statement

AFLAMEii has four main goals. First and foremost, AFLAMEii provides funding for diagnosis and treatment of adults above age 26 who are fighting lyme disease, and provides funding for diagnosis and treatment of people (all ages) who are fighting mold toxicity (biotoxin illness/chronic inflammatory response syndrome (CIRS)), and/or environmental intolerance (EI)/multiple chemical sensitivity (MCS). Concurrent with providing funding for diagnosis and treatment, AFLAMEii also pairs patients with advocates who are well-versed in these conditions, complications, and navigating the healthcare system. Lyme disease, mold toxicity (biotoxin illness/CIRS), and EI/MCS often co-occur, therefore AFLAMEii addresses all three.

Second, AFLAMEii serves to educate healthcare practitioners and the general population about lyme disease, mold toxicity (biotoxin illness/CIRS), and EI/MCS. Symptoms are often missed, or misdiagnosed as other conditions, and therefore are inappropriately treated. Improving awareness amongst healthcare providers and the general population is critical for reducing the impact of these conditions on our global community.

Third, AFLAMEii supports researchers, especially early-career researchers and doctoral students, who are interested in conducting research on lyme disease, mold toxicity/biotoxin illness/CIRS, and/or MCS/EI.

Fourth, AFLAMEii aims to partner with building developers and to construct low-toxin, mold-resistant homes and apartments both for individuals suffering from lyme disease, mold toxicity/biotoxin illness/CIRS, and MCS/EI, and for members of the general population who are interested in less-toxic living.
